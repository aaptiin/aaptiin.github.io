The Digital Public Lab

We are working on addressing questions of access, negotiation and governance for the digital public sector.

Idea of the public is vital to our thinking. Rights-based thinking, and the criticality of civil society are all strands underpinning this idea

We are a lab. We aim to learn and build right approaches, using the lab as a hub of learning

We are interested in digital technologies across several domains - we are keenly aware of their potential to impact human lives

so, what’s the problem?

We are witnessing loss of agency due to gaps in access and awareness

In an increasingly digital world, there is a rising need to address the significant gaps in access between those who have digital infrastructure and literacy, and those who do not. Deploying digital solutions for both public and private processes without acknowledging the realities of those it seeks to serve further deepens existing disparities in access.

our approach

To design digital solutions informed by people’s offline realities

We hope to arrive at approaches to enhance use, amplify accountability and create state capacity. We plan to do this by using offline architectures.

our focus areas

We prioritize by working with existing offline architecture or intermediaries and streamline their needs

Social mechanisms or structures (not technological) that work around technology. Offline architectures are a set of people, processes, and mechanisms that sit between powerful institutions and human beneficiaries, to bridge gaps in capacity, and build access to services. They exist alongside often digitised processes and delivery of services. In a democratic system, offline architectures play the important role of bridging critical access for populations that often lack it.

Our research so far

From our field data and coding, we attempted to arrive at a typology of intermediaries, with differential incentives, that could be engaged to better serve last-mile citizens in a given state or social context.

Awareness

Our last mile research pointed us towards breakdowns for access and the need for offline architectures in building the gap.

Offline architectures build trust
Offline architectures such as human intermediaries help build trust in institutions for those at the last-mile

Offline architectures contribute contextual knowledge
Offline architectures understand the capacity breakdowns and build the ability and agency for those who need it.

Governance

Our research on platform governance led us to include offline architectures to account for governance failures

Offline architectures to build accountability
Offline architectures should play significant and formal roles in governance in digital architectures serving society, thus enabling accountability

Offline architectures to empower the agency
Offline architectures are important for amplifying key interactions to enhance agency to collaborate with digital architectures.

Stakeholders

Our research has highlighted the need for coming of the three primary stakeholders - samaaj ( community), sarkaar (government), bazaar (market) coming together for co-creating solutions for societal problems.

Our early insights on access and governance can provide the foundation to build pathways for solutions to emerge.

What the lab aims to do?

Through research, dialogue, and analysis, we seek to understand and build ways to learn from human and analog mechanisms or offline architectures that support equitable digital access for the most vulnerable pockets of society.
